Surya Namaskara - Sun Salutation

Namaskara, or Sun Salutation, is a sequence of yoga poses performed in a continuous flow. It is a holistic exercise that provides numerous benefits for both the body and mind.

Benefits of Surya Namaskara:

  • Improves flexibility and strength
  • Boosts blood circulation
  • Enhances mental clarity and focus
  • Helps in weight loss and toning
  • Relieves stress and anxiety
  • Stimulates digestion and detoxification

How to Perform Surya Namaskara:

Surya Namaskara, also known as Sun Salutation, is a sequence of yoga poses that are performed in a flowing manner. Here are the steps for performing Surya Namaskara:

  1. Pranamasana (Prayer Pose)

    2. Pranamasana (Prayer Pose): Stand at the front of your mat with your feet together, palms pressed together in front of your chest, and breathe deeply.

    1. Stand tall with feet together, arms relaxed by the sides of the body.
    2. Bring the palms together in front of the chest in a prayer position (Anjali Mudra), with fingers pointing upwards.
    3. Keep the spine erect, shoulders relaxed, and gaze forward.
    4. Focus on breathing deeply and cultivating a sense of gratitude and reverence.

  2. Hastauttanasana (Raised Arms Pose)

    3. Hastauttanasana (Raised Arms Pose): Inhale as you raise your arms above your head, arching your back slightly and looking up at your hands.

    1. Begin by standing tall with your feet hip-width apart and your arms resting at your sides.
    2. Inhale deeply as you raise your arms overhead, keeping them parallel to each other and shoulder-width apart.
    3. Extend your arms fully with your palms facing each other or touching.
    4. Lengthen your spine upwards while grounding your feet firmly into the floor.
    5. Engage your core muscles and relax your shoulders away from your ears.

  3. Hasta Padasana (Hand to Foot Pose)

    4. Hasta Padasana (Hand to Foot Pose): Exhale as you bend forward from the hips, keeping your spine straight, and bring your hands down to the floor beside your feet. You can bend your knees if necessary.

    1. Stand tall with your feet hip-width apart, toes pointing forward, and arms resting by your sides.
    2. Keep your spine straight and engage your core muscles for stability.
    3. Inhale deeply as you raise your arms overhead, palms facing each other.
    4. Exhale slowly as you hinge forward at your hips, maintaining a straight back.
    5. Reach your hands down towards your feet or shins, keeping your knees slightly bent if necessary to avoid strain.
    6. Once you've reached your maximum stretch, hold the position for several breaths, allowing your torso to gently relax closer to your thighs.
    7. Keep your neck in line with your spine and your gaze directed towards your legs.

  4. Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Equestrian Pose)

    5. Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Equestrian Pose): Inhale and step your right leg back into a lunge position, keeping your left knee bent at a 90-degree angle and your hands on the floor beside your left foot. Look up and keep your chest open.

    1. Inhale deeply and step your right foot back, keeping your toes tucked under and your heel lifted.
    2. Simultaneously, bend your left knee, ensuring it aligns directly over your ankle.
    3. Your right leg should be extended behind you, with your knee slightly lifted off the ground.
    4. Exhale as you lower your right knee towards the floor, ensuring your hips are squared forward.
    5. Sink into the stretch, feeling a gentle opening in the front of your right hip and thigh.
    6. Keep your chest lifted and shoulders relaxed.
    7. Hold the pose for a few breaths, maintaining stability and balance.

  5. Chaturanga Dandasana (Stick Pose)

    6. Chaturanga Dandasana (Stick Pose): As you exhale, step your left leg back to meet your right leg, coming into a plank position. Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.

    1. Begin by positioning yourself on the floor face down, with your palms flat on the ground directly under your shoulders, fingers spread wide.
    2. Engage your core muscles by drawing your navel towards your spine.
    3. Extend your legs straight back, keeping them hip-width apart and pressing through your heels.
    4. Your body should form a straight line from your head to your heels, with your hips neither sagging nor lifted too high.
    5. Maintain steady breathing as you hold the pose, keeping your gaze slightly forward and your neck in line with your spine.

  6. Ashtanga Namaskara (Eight-Limbed Pose)

    7. Ashtanga Namaskara (Eight-Limbed Pose): Lower your knees, chest, and chin to the floor while keeping your hips lifted. Your elbows should be close to your body. Only your hands, feet, knees, chest, and chin should touch the ground.

    1. Begin in the plank pose, with your palms firmly pressed into the ground, shoulders aligned over your wrists, and your body forming a straight line from head to heels.
    2. Engage your core muscles to maintain stability.
    3. Slowly lower your body down towards the ground in a controlled manner, keeping your elbows close to your ribs.
    4. As you lower, bend your elbows and lower your chest and chin to touch the ground simultaneously.
    5. Keep your hips lifted slightly, and ensure your body remains in a straight line.
    6. Once your body is lowered, the eight points of contact with the ground should be your chin, chest, two palms, two knees, and two toes. Your buttocks should be slightly raised.

  7. Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose)

    8. Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose): Inhale as you slide forward and raise your chest and head off the floor, keeping your elbows bent and close to your body. Use your back muscles to lift.

    1. Lie down on your stomach with your legs extended and feet hip-width apart.
    2. Place your palms on the floor under your shoulders, fingers pointing forward, and elbows close to your body.
    3. Inhale deeply and slowly lift your chest off the floor, keeping your elbows bent and close to your sides.
    4. Press into your palms to lift your upper body, while keeping your pelvis and legs firmly grounded.
    5. As you lift, engage your core muscles and lengthen through your spine, gently arching your back.
    6. Keep your shoulders relaxed away from your ears and gaze forward.
    7. Hold the pose for a few breaths, then exhale as you slowly release back down to the floor.

  8. Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward-Facing Dog Pose)

    9. Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward-Facing Dog Pose): Exhale as you lift your hips and tailbone toward the ceiling, coming into an inverted V-shape. Press your heels down toward the floor and straighten your arms and legs.

    1. Start in a tabletop position on your hands and knees, ensuring your wrists are directly under your shoulders and your knees are under your hips.
    2. Exhale and lift your hips up and back, straightening your arms and legs to form an inverted V shape with your body.
    3. Keep your hands shoulder-width apart and your feet hip-width apart.
    4. Press your palms firmly into the ground, lengthen your spine, and engage your core as you actively press your heels towards the floor.
    5. Hold the pose for several breaths, focusing on lengthening your spine and relaxing your neck and shoulders.

  9. Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Equestrian Pose)

    10. Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Equestrian Pose): Inhale as you step your right foot forward between your hands, returning to the lunge position.

    1. Inhale deeply, engaging your core muscles and lifting your torso gently.
    2. As you continue to inhale, slowly step your right foot forward, returning it to its original position beside the left foot.
    3. Ensure your weight is evenly distributed between both feet.
    4. Once your right foot is firmly planted, align it with your left foot, maintaining the hip-width distance.
    5. Exhale as you extend your spine, lengthening it upward while relaxing your shoulders down.
    6. Allow your arms to rest alongside your body, palms facing inward or gently placed on your thighs.

  10. Hasta Padasana (Hand to Foot Pose)

    11. Hasta Padasana (Hand to Foot Pose): Exhale as you bend forward from the hips, keeping your spine straight, and bring your hands down to the floor beside your feet. You can bend your knees if necessary.

    1. Stand tall with your feet hip-width apart, toes pointing forward, and arms resting by your sides.
    2. Keep your spine straight and engage your core muscles for stability.
    3. Inhale deeply as you raise your arms overhead, palms facing each other.
    4. Exhale slowly as you hinge forward at your hips, maintaining a straight back.
    5. Reach your hands down towards your feet or shins, keeping your knees slightly bent if necessary to avoid strain.
    6. Once you've reached your maximum stretch, hold the position for several breaths, allowing your torso to gently relax closer to your thighs.
    7. Keep your neck in line with your spine and your gaze directed towards your legs.

  11. Hastauttanasana (Raised Arms Pose)

    12. Hastauttanasana (Raised Arms Pose): Inhale as you raise your arms above your head, arching your back slightly and looking up at your hands.

    1. Begin by standing tall with your feet hip-width apart and your arms resting at your sides.
    2. Inhale deeply as you raise your arms overhead, keeping them parallel to each other and shoulder-width apart.
    3. Extend your arms fully with your palms facing each other or touching.
    4. Lengthen your spine upwards while grounding your feet firmly into the floor.
    5. Engage your core muscles and relax your shoulders away from your ears.

  12. Pranamasana (Prayer Pose)

    13. Pranamasana (Prayer Pose): Stand at the front of your mat with your feet together, palms pressed together in front of your chest, and breathe deeply.

    1. Stand tall with feet together, arms relaxed by the sides of the body.
    2. Bring the palms together in front of the chest in a prayer position (Anjali Mudra), with fingers pointing upwards.
    3. Keep the spine erect, shoulders relaxed, and gaze forward.
    4. Focus on breathing deeply and cultivating a sense of gratitude and reverence.

    Repeat the sequence, starting with the opposite leg in step 4. Aim for 5-10 rounds initially, gradually increasing as you become more comfortable with the flow.
