Surya Namaskara - Sun Salutation

Namaskara, or Sun Salutation, is a sequence of yoga poses performed in a continuous flow. It is a holistic exercise that provides numerous benefits for both the body and mind.

Benefits of Surya Namaskara:

  • Improves flexibility and strength
  • Boosts blood circulation
  • Enhances mental clarity and focus
  • Helps in weight loss and toning
  • Relieves stress and anxiety
  • Stimulates digestion and detoxification

How to Perform Surya Namaskara:

  1. Start in the Mountain Pose (Tadasana).
  2. Raise your arms overhead in the Raised Arms Pose (Urdhva Hastasana).
  3. Bend forward into the Standing Forward Bend (Uttanasana).
  4. Step your right leg back into a lunge, transitioning into the Low Lunge (Anjaneyasana).
  5. Step your left leg back to plank position, then lower down into the Four-Limbed Staff Pose (Chaturanga Dandasana).
  6. Lower your hips to the floor and lift your chest into the Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana).
  7. Push back into the Downward-Facing Dog Pose (Adho Mukha Svanasana).
  8. Step your right foot forward into a lunge, transitioning back into the Low Lunge (Anjaneyasana).
  9. Step your left foot forward to meet your right foot in the Standing Forward Bend (Uttanasana).
  10. Raise your arms overhead again in the Raised Arms Pose (Urdhva Hastasana).
  11. Return to the Mountain Pose (Tadasana).

Repeat the sequence, starting with the opposite leg in step 4. Aim for 5-10 rounds initially, gradually increasing as you become more comfortable with the flow.
